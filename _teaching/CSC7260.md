<!-- Description -->
<div class="cs-section">
<div class="cs-section-title">Course Description</div>
<p style="font-size:0.93em;color:#333;line-height:1.7;">
This course covers both basic distributed computing ideas and system designs that support distributed applications. Students develop a thorough understanding of the <strong>fundamentals of distributed computing</strong>, learn to construct distributed system components, and comprehend the current state of the art across various areas of distributed systems - including abstract concepts and practical methods for creating system support for distributed applications.
</p>
</div>

<!-- Objectives -->
<div class="cs-section">
<div class="cs-section-title">Course Objectives</div>
<div class="obj-grid">
  <div class="obj-card">
    <div class="obj-card-title">Core Concepts</div>
    <div class="obj-card-desc">Understand logical clocks, consistent cuts, consensus, replication, and fault tolerance in distributed systems.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="obj-card">
    <div class="obj-card-title">Implementation Skills</div>
    <div class="obj-card-desc">Learn standard techniques to build correct and performant distributed systems and applications.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="obj-card">
    <div class="obj-card-title">Practical Systems</div>
    <div class="obj-card-desc">Be familiar with multiple practical distributed systems underpinning popular real-world applications and services.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="obj-card">
    <div class="obj-card-title">Principled Design</div>
    <div class="obj-card-desc">Know how systems are realized by relying on core distributed computing concepts and design patterns.</div>
  </div>
</div>
</div>

<!-- Topics -->
<div class="cs-section">
<div class="cs-section-title">Topics</div>
<div class="topic-grid">
  <div class="topic-card">
    <div class="topic-card-header">Foundations</div>
    <ul>
      <li>Introduction: definitions, design goals, types</li>
      <li>Architectures: styles, middleware, system</li>
      <li>Processes: threads, virtualization, clients, servers</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="topic-card">
    <div class="topic-card-header">Communication &amp; Naming</div>
    <ul>
      <li>Communication: foundation, remote call, message-oriented</li>
      <li>Naming: flat, structured, and attribute-based approaches</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="topic-card">
    <div class="topic-card-header">Coordination &amp; Consistency</div>
    <ul>
      <li>Coordination: clock sync, election algorithm</li>
      <li>Consistency and replication: client-centric models, replica management</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="topic-card">
    <div class="topic-card-header">Reliability &amp; Security</div>
    <ul>
      <li>Fault tolerance: process resilience, reliable communication, recovery</li>
      <li>Security: secure channel, access control, secure naming</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
</div>
