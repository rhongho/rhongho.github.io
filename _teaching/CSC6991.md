<div class="cs-section">
<div class="cs-section-title">Course Description</div>
<p style="font-size:0.93em;color:#333;line-height:1.7;">
The next-generation hybrid cloud platform is integrated with Network Functions (NFs) across diverse network environments, ushering in a new computing paradigm known as <strong>in-network computing (INC)</strong>. This introductory course offers system and network engineers the opportunity to engage in large-scale system optimization - learning practical <strong>data plane programming</strong> skills, gaining hands-on experience in hardware programming using software techniques, and exploring real-world applications through case studies in data centers, distributed AI/ML systems, security, and more.
</p>
</div>

<!-- Industry Trends -->
<div class="cs-section">
<div class="cs-section-title">Industry Trends &amp; Why INC Matters Now</div>
<p style="font-size:0.88em;color:#444;line-height:1.6;margin-bottom:16px;">
  In-Network Computing is no longer a research curiosity - it is actively shaping how the world's largest tech companies build AI infrastructure. Here are the latest developments:
</p>
<div style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:repeat(auto-fill,minmax(260px,1fr));gap:16px;">

  <div style="background:#fff;border:1px solid #e0e0e0;border-top:3px solid #1565c0;border-radius:8px;padding:16px;font-size:0.84em;">
    <div style="font-weight:700;color:#1565c0;margin-bottom:6px;">NVIDIA BlueField-4 DPU &mdash; GTC 2025</div>
    <div style="color:#444;line-height:1.6;">NVIDIA unveiled BlueField-4, delivering <strong>800 Gb/s throughput</strong> and 6x the compute of its predecessor. It handles networking, storage, security, and AI orchestration entirely off the host CPU. Nearly <strong>50% of cloud providers</strong> now rely on DPUs - Oracle, CoreWeave, xAI, and Lambda are already building on it.</div>
  </div>

  <div style="background:#fff;border:1px solid #e0e0e0;border-top:3px solid #1565c0;border-radius:8px;padding:16px;font-size:0.84em;">
    <div style="font-weight:700;color:#1565c0;margin-bottom:6px;">Meta Open Programmable Fabric for AI &mdash; Oct. 2024</div>
    <div style="color:#444;line-height:1.6;">Meta revealed its Disaggregated Scheduled Fabric (DSF) at OCP Summit 2024 - a vendor-agnostic, open programmable network for AI training clusters. By 2025, it scaled to a <strong>2-stage architecture supporting 18,432 XPUs</strong>, powered by a <strong>51-Terabit Minipack3N</strong> switch for their largest AI clusters.</div>
  </div>

  <div style="background:#fff;border:1px solid #e0e0e0;border-top:3px solid #1565c0;border-radius:8px;padding:16px;font-size:0.84em;">
    <div style="font-weight:700;color:#1565c0;margin-bottom:6px;">Microsoft Azure Boost SmartNIC &mdash; Deployed 2024</div>
    <div style="color:#444;line-height:1.6;">Microsoft's Azure Boost (FPGA-based SmartNIC) offloads all networking and storage virtualization from the host CPU - achieving <strong>12.5 GB/s throughput</strong>, <strong>650K IOPS</strong>, and <strong>sub-15 microsecond VM-to-VM latency</strong>. Deployed across <strong>1 million+ Azure servers</strong> with all future VMs planned to use it.</div>
  </div>

  <div style="background:#fff;border:1px solid #e0e0e0;border-top:3px solid #1565c0;border-radius:8px;padding:16px;font-size:0.84em;">
    <div style="font-weight:700;color:#1565c0;margin-bottom:6px;">Google Jupiter: 13 Petabits/sec Datacenter Fabric &mdash; 2024</div>
    <div style="color:#444;line-height:1.6;">Google's Jupiter network fabric now scales to <strong>13 Pb/s of bisection bandwidth</strong> - a 5x capacity improvement - using software-defined networking and Optical Circuit Switches (OCS) to dynamically reconfigure topology for AI/ML workloads. This backbone powers Google's <strong>TPU v4 AI supercomputer</strong> clusters.</div>
  </div>
